--- layout: post title: SAS L is StiLL aLive date: '2017-05-12T04:00:00.000-07:00' author: Allan Bowe tags: modified_time: '2017-05-12T04:01:42.240-07:00' thumbnail: https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Cvvapp2ZVQo/WRWUsjVfvUI/AAAAAAAAA0w/AHH5n41d8xM1q_hWyDur4MrjS9vyPGSEgCLcB/s72-c/Screen%2BShot%2B2017-05-11%2Bat%2B23.52.27.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3488921339026456633.post-8406073334004946566 blogger_orig_url: http://www.rawsas.com/2017/05/sas-l-is-still-alive.html --- If you've ever done any moderate googling on a SAS topic you've probably unearthed a thread on SAS-L, quite possibly from several decades ago.  You'd be forgiven for assuming that this simply something that "used to exist' because - up until recently - that's exactly what I thought too.

Well - how wrong I was.  SAS-L is still alive and kicking.  The "L" stands for listserv, and is a mail server run by the University of Georgia.  It's basically a spamming service for SAS issues (am sure there is a better word for it though).

Getting started is as easy as 1,2,3:

1 - Sign up to the listserv service: https://listserv.uga.edu/cgi-bin/wa?GETPW1
2 - Verify your email
3 - Subscribe to SAS-L  mailing list: https://listserv.uga.edu/cgi-bin/wa?SUBED1=sas-l



There is a ton of information on SAS-L on sasCommunity, and even a dedicated sugi paper.  So get involved - and you could be the next Rookie of the Year!
